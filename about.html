The argument for solo

Why one person, not a team.

No handoffs, no telephone game.

The classic agency setup is account manager → strategist → designer → developer. By the time something reaches whoever's actually building, the original idea has been paraphrased four times.

Solo means the person who hears the brief is the person who ships the result. Nothing gets lost in the relay.

Tighter feedback loops.

You email me Tuesday morning. I read it Tuesday morning. The conversation moves at the speed of email, not at the speed of a Monday status meeting.

For brand-led work where every detail compounds, that compounds in your favour.

One bill, one accountability.

No subcontractors invoicing through you, no "that wasn't in the designer's scope." Single setup fee, single monthly, single person who answers when something breaks.

Smaller in headcount, not in capability.

Web. Brand. Game-dev. Product. Multi-disciplinary by choice, not by stretching. The integration tax of coordinating four freelancers is real — solo skips it entirely.

The argument for brand-led

Why brand-led, not template-led.

Most agencies template the design and customise the content.

Familiar layout, familiar grid, familiar hero. The "brand voice" lives in copy and a colour swap. The visitor's brain pattern-matches it as 'another agency site'.

Cardinal Studio does the opposite — bespoke design driven by the brand. The site looks like the brand, not like a template carrying the brand.

I work best with brands worth being a fan of.

Gaming studios. Niche IP with active community. Owner-led businesses where the owner cares about the product. Anywhere the brand has texture and the audience picks up on it.

Being a fan of the work is half the work — it's why my favourite cases are projects I'd happily use myself.

Honest disclosure

Where Cardinal Studio is NOT a fit.

Big SaaS dashboards or enterprise ecommerce.

Custom data-heavy product UI, complex auth, payment processing at scale — all real, all specialised. There are studios that do this brilliantly. I'm not one of them.

Five-stakeholder approval chains.

If your project needs marketing + brand + comms + legal + procurement to sign off every wireframe, the speed advantage of solo evaporates. You'd be paying me to wait. Be honest with yourself; I'll be honest with you.

"Just make it look like [Company X]."

Cardinal Studio is brand-led. Reskinning a competitor's site is the opposite of that. If that's what the project needs, you want a different studio.

Anything with "transformação digital" in the brief.

If the language sounds like a McKinsey deck, I'm not your person. Cardinal Studio talks like a builder, not a consultant.
